# QuickBooks Payments — How to Apply (Canada)

## The short version

You apply for **QuickBooks Payments** (also called Merchant Services) from inside your
QuickBooks Online account. It is **not** an instant "turn it on" toggle — you fill out an
application, Intuit reviews it, and approval takes a few business days (longer if they ask
for extra documents). This is the account that lets clients pay the invoice online.

**Confirmed:** it's a genuine application you can complete yourself, it goes to underwriting
review, and it takes time — exactly what you expected.

## Where to go

- Sign in to QuickBooks Online → **Settings (gear)** → **Account and Settings** → **Payments**,
  and choose **Learn more / Set up Payments**.
- Or go directly to the Canada Payments page: https://quickbooks.intuit.com/ca/payments/

## What you'll fill out

- **Business details** — legal business name, business address, and what the charges are for
  (describe the appraisal service clearly — they want this detailed).
- **Your identity** — name, residential address, and SIN (for the underwriting/credit check).
- **Deposit account** — the business bank account where paid funds land.

## What they may ask for after you submit

If the application goes to "pending review," Intuit can request:

- A **utility bill** to verify the address against their credit-agency data.
- Up to **3 months of business bank statements** to confirm your expected processing volume.

Having these ready speeds it up.

## Timeline

- **Approval:** a few business days for a clean application; longer if documents are requested.
- **First deposits after approval:** the first payment takes a bit longer while the account
  finishes setup, then card payments settle in roughly a couple of business days.

## Start this early

The merchant approval is the one slow, you-only step in the whole pay-on-signing flow — the
software side can't go live until the account is approved. Kicking off the application is the
green light for the rest to get wired.
